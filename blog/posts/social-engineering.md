# Hacking the Human Firewall

Social engineering relies on human interaction to trick people into breaking security procedures. It is often the easiest way to breach a secure network, as it targets the "human element" rather than technical vulnerabilities.

## Common Techniques

1.  **Phishing:** Sending fraudulent emails that appear to come from a reputable source.
2.  **Pretexting:** Creating a fabricated scenario (a pretext) to steal a victim's personal information.
3.  **Baiting:** Offering something enticing (like a free download or a USB drive left in a parking lot) to an end-user in exchange for private data or access.
4.  **Quid Pro Quo:** Requesting information in exchange for a service or benefit (e.g., pretending to be IT support).

## Warning Signs

*   **Urgency:** Attackers often create a false sense of urgency ("Act now or your account will be locked").
*   **Unexpected Requests:** Requests for passwords or sensitive data via email or phone.
*   **Suspicious Links:** URLs that look slightly wrong (e.g., `g00gle.com`).

## Defense

Security awareness training is the most effective defense. Always verify the identity of the person requesting information and never click on suspicious links.
